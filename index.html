Mars Landscape
Overall, the landscape of Mars is a fascinating and varied terrain that has been shaped by a combination of geological and climatic processes over billions of years.The landscape of Mars is characterized by a variety of features that have been shaped by geological processes over billions of years. Some of the most prominent features on Mars include impact craters, volcanoes, canyons, and vast plains.

In the context of space exploration, a "landing" generally refers to the process of safely bringing a spacecraft down to the surface of a planet or other celestial body. JPL has been involved in the development and operation of a number of such lander missions, including the Mars Pathfinder mission in 1996, the Mars Exploration Rovers (Spirit and Opportunity) in 2004, the Mars Phoenix lander in 2008, and the Mars Science Laboratory (Curiosity rover) in 2012. JPL is also currently involved in the development of the Mars 2020 mission, which includes the Perseverance rover and the Ingenuity Mars helicopter.
